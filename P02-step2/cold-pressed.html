Why Cold-pressed?

Cold Pressed is a method of juicing using a Cold Pressed Juicer. This involves a 2-step process of grinding and pressing the produce which extracts juice without generating heat. Cold Pressed Juicers press the produce, causing minimal oxidation therefore allowing the juice to stay fresh longer.
Centrifugal Juicer (what most people have at home and in some juice bars, cafés, etc.) spins rapidly which creates heat that destroys vitamins, minerals and enzymes. Centrifugal’s blades rip apart the produce very quickly causing the juice to mix with air. This method oxidizes the juice, making the molecules degrade faster and resulting in a shorter shelf life. Cold Pressed juice will last up to 4 days in the fridge, whereas a Centrifugal juice should be consumed immediately to reap the most nutritional benefits.
